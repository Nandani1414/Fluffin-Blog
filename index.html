Sparrow and their eggs. The house bird.

The sparrow is a small, friendly bird commonly found in cities and villages. Sparrows are known for their cheerful chirping and close presence around humans. They usually lay 3 to 5 small white or pale grey eggs, sometimes speckled with brown. The female sparrow incubates the eggs for about 11 to 14 days, and both parents feed the chicks after hatching. Sparrow eggs are tiny and delicate, just like the bird — a symbol of simplicity and warmth in nature.



peacock and their eggs. The dancing jewel of nature.

The peacock is a beautiful bird known for its colorful tail feathers. It is famous for spreading its feathers in a fan shape during a special dance. Peacocks are mostly blue and green and are very eye-catching. The female peacock is called a peahen. She lays 3 to 6 eggs in a nest on the ground. The eggs are light brown or cream-colored and hatch after about 28 days



Owl and its eggs. Night Bird.

The owl is a bird known for being active at night and having big round eyes. Owls can turn their heads almost all the way around and are very quiet flyers. They are great hunters and eat small animals like mice and insects. The female owl lays 2 to 6 eggs in a nest, which is often found in tree holes or abandoned buildings. Owl eggs are white and round, and the mother keeps them warm until they hatch.



Hummingbird - . The Tiny Flying Jewel.

The hummingbird is a tiny, colorful bird known for its ability to hover in mid-air by flapping its wings super fast—up to 80 times per second! It gets its name from the humming sound its wings make when flying. The female hummingbird lays 2 tiny eggs, each the size of a pea or jellybean, in a small cup-shaped nest made of soft materials like plant fluff and spider silk. These are among the smallest eggs in the bird world.
